# Definition
A dipole is any object which has a charge differential across it, creating a positive;y-charged side and a negatively-charged side of that object. Every dipole has a quantity called *dipole moment* which measures how drastically it is polarized, and all dipoles create electric fields due to the separation of charges.

Many molecules are dipoles, since they have fixed structures that place two oppositely-charged ions apart from each other. All atoms become dipoles when in the presence of an external electric field, since the atom's nucleus is pulled in the opposite direction to its electron cloud.
