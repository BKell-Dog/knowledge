

## Current Density
Current density is a measure of the amount of charge per unit of time that flows through the unit area of a given cross section.

Suppose we have some closed surface $S$ with an area equal to $A$. Suppose that there is a current $I$, that is, a quantity of charge moving through $S$. First, if we suppose that all these charges move orthogonally to the plane of $S$, and that the current is DC and constant, we have simply,
$$
\vec{J}=\frac{\vec{I}}{A}=\frac{I}{A}\hat{n}
$$
where $\hat{n}$ is the unit vector normal to $S$.

If the current is AC or changes through time, $\vec{I}(t)$, and if the charge moves at an angle to the surface $S$, then we can consider an infinitesimal current traveling through an infinitesimal area,
$$
d\vec{I}=\vec{J} \cdot d\vec{A}
$$
then we can rewrite this equation as,
$$
\vec{I}=\int_S\vec{J}\>\cdot\>d\vec{S}
$$
This is the form of the equation you will see most often. It is relatively unusual to find the $\vec{J}$ term isolated on its own.
